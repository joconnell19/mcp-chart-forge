Let me walk through how the money actually flows for MacHub. Every dollar of gross profit goes to SCOG. 70% funds MacHub operations -- that's labor, supplies, everything it takes to run production. SCOG retains the remaining 30%. Out of that 30%, Swedemom LLC takes a 6% management fee -- so $1.80 on every $100. The remaining 94%, or $28.20, stays with SCOG for the mission.

External hubs flip the split. Using Blanchet House as an example with a 30% partner share -- SCOG keeps 70% instead of 30%. The same 6% LLC fee applies to SCOG's portion, so Swedemom LLC gets $4.20 and SCOG nets $65.80 on every $100. That's more than double what SCOG nets per sale from MacHub. This is why scaling through external hubs is so attractive for the mission.

Looking ahead. Q2 is packed. The Pack Station brings packing and shipping directly into the hub workflow -- this should cut fulfillment labor costs and potentially free up resources we can shift toward production. Camera-enabled AI sorting will use computer vision to speed up intake categorization significantly. The new Manage Listings tool with a relisting agent will automate work that Annika and Cami currently do manually. We're also improving our pricing agents, pushing the Final Check agent from 75% to 95% coverage, reducing AWS costs, building a new SCOG site to better leverage our Google Ads Grant, overhauling swedemom.com for more traffic, and adding customer support tooling into the hub. In Q3 and Q4, we expand to additional marketplaces beyond eBay, overhaul payment processing, continue improving our AI tools, build full casual hub support for non-local partners, enhance our external API, add AI customer service agents, and fully retire all legacy hub and intranet pages.
